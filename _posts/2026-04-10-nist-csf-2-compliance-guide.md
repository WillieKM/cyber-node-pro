## // FRAMEWORK UPDATE

The National Institute of Standards and Technology released **NIST CSF 2.0** — the first major revision since the original 2014 framework. If your organization references NIST for compliance, contracts, or client requirements, this affects you.

Here's what changed and what you need to do.

---

## The Big Change: A 6th Core Function

CSF 1.1 had 5 functions: Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover.

CSF 2.0 adds a 6th: **Govern**.

The Govern function covers organizational context, risk management strategy, cybersecurity supply chain risk, and roles and responsibilities. It sits above the other five as the foundation that drives all security decisions.

---

## Other Key Changes

**Expanded scope** — CSF 2.0 is no longer just for critical infrastructure. It's explicitly designed for organizations of all sizes and sectors.

**Supply chain risk** — Significantly expanded guidance on managing third-party and vendor security risk (formerly an afterthought).

**Implementation Examples** — The new framework includes concrete examples for each subcategory, making it far easier to operationalize.

**Tiers updated** — The four tiers (Partial, Risk Informed, Repeatable, Adaptive) now apply specifically to governance practices.

---

## Quick Compliance Checklist

Use this to assess your current posture against CSF 2.0:

**Govern**
- [ ] Documented cybersecurity risk management strategy
- [ ] Defined roles and responsibilities for security
- [ ] Third-party/vendor risk program in place

**Identify**
- [ ] Full asset inventory (hardware, software, data)
- [ ] Risk assessment conducted within last 12 months

**Protect**
- [ ] MFA enforced across critical systems
- [ ] Least-privilege access controls implemented
- [ ] Security awareness training active

**Detect**
- [ ] Log monitoring and SIEM in place
- [ ] Anomaly detection configured

**Respond**
- [ ] Incident response plan documented and tested
- [ ] Communication plan for stakeholders

**Recover**
- [ ] Backup and recovery procedures tested
- [ ] Recovery objectives defined (RTO/RPO)
